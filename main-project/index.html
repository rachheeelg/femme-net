Femme*Net is a public archive to celebrate the women in technology. With a focus on the past, present, and future, our goal is to make feminist internet history accessible to all. This is a love letter to the women who had a hand in building the modern internet. It is also for the next generation of women, a record for all of us to continue building a more equitable internet where women's perspectives are represented.

Ants are one of nature's most enduring symbols of collective intelligence and of invisible labor made visible. Each ant follows its own path, leaves its own trail, and yet together they build something far greater than any individual could alone. No single ant is credited. No single ant leads. And still, the colony finds its way.

Femme*Net draws on this same truth. The women who built the internet rarely had their names in the headlines. They worked in the background, laid the groundwork, and left trails that others followed, often without knowing it. This archive is an act of making those trails visible.

The ant is not just a symbol of efficiency or optimization. Here, it is a symbol of collective, uncredited, essential work and of what becomes possible when we finally start tracing those paths back to the women who made them.

This project started as my way of healing through a fuck-ass situationship with a tech bro that low-key ruined my perception of how data is treated on the internet. I had many evil thoughts about that man, which I worked through with my therapist, but I digress.

That experience sparked an idea: what if the internet were run by women? In this age of automation and AI, what does it mean to be a cyberfeminist in the 21st century? What resources can women access, and how do we acknowledge the work women have done across the past, present, and future?

When VNS Matrix introduced cyberfeminism, their manifesto imagined an internet that could be rewritten and reclaimed. Femme*Net builds on that same question, but through an archive.
